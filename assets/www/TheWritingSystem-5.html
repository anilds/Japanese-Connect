The Writing System

After World War II, laws were passed that limited the Kanji that could be used in newspapers to a fixed set of about 2000 characters. Everything else was to be represented in Hiragana and Katakana. So, although it is much easier to be literate in Japan now than in the past, you still do have to know Kanji.

We won't spend a lot of time learning Kanji, because as you can imagine, they're really a field of study unto themselves. But I will present them from time to time so that you understand how they fit into Japanese. Then later, you'll be able to make a more detailed study of them if you decide to do so.
