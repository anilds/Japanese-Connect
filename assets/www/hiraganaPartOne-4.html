Hiragana, Part-1

Ten-ten

Moving along, here's the next set of characters, called the k-series because each character begins with a "k" sound. Again, notice the hane on several of the characters, and be sure to include those when

It's tough to draw these characters with a ballpoint pen. Use a real wood, number two pencil and draw them largely. Don't try to fit them on ruled paper just yet. Take your time and write them over and over, while saying the pronunciation out loud. This will really help sink them into your brain.

So by applying ten-ten to the five characters in the k-series, we essentially create another series, the "g-series," comprised of the sounds ga, gi, gu, ge, and go.
