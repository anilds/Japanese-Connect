Hiragana, Part-1

k-series

Here are the next five characters in the Hiragana syllabary, the s-series. You might notice right away that shi appears to be the odd man out. Believe it or not, there is no sound "si" in the Japanese language.

You'll notice that sa looks very similar to ki from our last series, but with only one horizontal stroke instead of two. Just like ki, this character often appears in printing with the second and third strokes connected, as at the right.
