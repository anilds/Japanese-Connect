Hiragana, Part-1

K-series

The k-series is different from the a-series in that with just five characters, it's actually possible to make ten sounds. How can this be? It happens by adding marks called ten-ten, which mean something like "mark-mark."

Ten-ten are technically called dakuten, or "softening marks." This is exactly what they do. When we add them to any character from the k-series, it becomes a "g" sound. Thus ka with ten-ten becomes ga. Ki becomes gi, and so on. Take a look at this visually:
