Now, I've told you that the vowels are always pronounced the same way. This isn't entirely true. There is one situation where a vowel may not be pronounced as I've shown on the above chart. That situation has to do with extending the sounds e and o. Those sounds are normally extended, not by writing another of the same vowel, as you would expect, but by writing i and u, respectively. In other words, to make an e sound long, you write an i after it, not another e. Likewise, to double an o sound, you add an u, not another o.
