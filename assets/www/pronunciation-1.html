Pronunciation

Starting from lesson two, we'll start learning the syllabaries. Until we've mastered them, we'll use both Roman letters and the syllabaries to represent the Japanese words we encounter. However, by lesson 18, we'll begin using the syllabaries exclusively. Although this may seem overwhelming right now, they are really easy to learn and understand. (Japanese also uses a third character set, called Kanji, which was borrowed from Chinese. We'll discuss Kanji a bit more in the next lesson.)

The "never" notes are just friendly reminders because as English-speakers, we often tend to allow letters that follow a vowel to influence the pronunciation of that vowel. For example, take the car company Honda. Americans always mispronounce the name because they change the o sound when they see the n after it. In Japanese, it is pronounced "hone-dah," because in Japanese, o is always pronounced "oh," regardless of what follows.
