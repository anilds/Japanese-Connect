The Writing System

The Chinese brought their characters to Korea, Vietnam, and Japan. Japan received Chinese characters around the year 400 AD, and soon thereafter, officials in Japan began writing Japanese using Chinese characters. The Japanese name for these characters is Kanji. There was just one problem. Japanese and Chinese are totally, completely different languages.

Imagine that we English speakers are living out our lives, going about our business, with no writing system. Perhaps we have crude calendars and some basic symbols, but certainly no means of writing something like a newspaper or grocery list. Now the Chinese show up at our doorstep and tell us about their fantastic writing system. It looks pretty good. The ability to be able to accurately record thoughts is fascinating. So we decide to give it a go.

At first everything seems fairly straightforward. To write down any sentence, you just find the Chinese character for each word and write it down in order. So the sentence "I like ramen" is easy. Just find the characters for each word, write them down, and now anyone can read it back in English and get the exact sentence you originally meant.

Make no mistake - at this point you are not writing Chinese - you are writing down English sentences using Chinese characters. Splendid!
