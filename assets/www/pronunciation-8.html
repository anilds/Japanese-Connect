Doubled Consonants

Doubled consonants work in much the same way as the doubled vowels. The concept is the same. When two of the same consonant appear next to each other, the consonant lasts for 2 "beats" instead of one. But how do you make a consonant last twice as long? You pause on the consonant for an extra beat. Therefore, in the word metta, you would pronounce me, then take your tongue up into position for the ta sound, but before doing that you freeze right there for one "beat." Finally, you say the ta sound. Try it and compare the sound to the version with no consonant doubling.

Meta Metta

When writing Japanese in Roman letters, consonants are doubled by writing the letter twice.
