Pronunciation

Let's listen to a few words pronounced the correct and incorrect way. The correct way is a recording of a native Japanese speaker. The incorrect way is a recording of me, pronouncing the word the way foreigners often do.

One trick to making sure that you don't stress a particular syllable is to make sure you don't change the pronunciation of any of the syllables. For example, when foreigners incorrectly stress the middle syllable of watashi, the first syllable is usually warped as a result, and the final word sounds like "wuh-TA-shi." But "wuh" is not a Japanese sound at all! So if you pay close attention and make sure you give each syllable the correct vowel sound, it'll go a long way to making sure you aren't unnecessarily stressing any syllables.

For example, if you see the word kaeru, you simply say "ka-e-ru." The e does not exercise magical powers over the a as it would in English. This is most definitely not pronounced "kay-ru." Just read the vowels the way they're always read.
